About

The guy in the above pic is Shrish-Tripathi.

A left-handed person, coordinated by the right hemisphere of the brain. A slow writer, a slow typist, but a keen thinker, with good observational skills. An unsociable guy but not a grumpy one.

Born in a typical north-Indian family, I am native to Ayodhya (the birthplace of Hindu god Rama). Though I spent my childhood in Delhi, and adolescence in Lucknow, I used to spend my summer vacation in my village, a beautiful village, whose foot is washed by the river Ghaghra, a tributary of the Ganga (locally Saryu). Far from the crowded hustle-bustle of cities, I used to enjoy the time on mother nature’s lap to purify my soul.

Coming to my educational life, I passed my matriculation from Faizabad Public School and my intermediate from Delhi Public School (D.P.S). Currently, I am pursuing my engineering from the Indian Institue of Technology (I.I.T) Ropar in Metallurgical and Materials Engineering. Wait -- what, engineering -- why?
Because I wanted to.
The motivation behind doing engineering?
I love the difficult tasks, I strive for things that seem to be unachievable. Sometimes I fail, sometimes I succeed but every time I get an unforgettable experience and learn something new. Engineering is all about higher-order thinking. It motivates you to think like a technocrat and learn new things.
Apart from that,I am stargazer and an astrogeek. I was fascinated by astronomy since childhood. I also worked as a coordinator of the astronomy club of IIT Ropar. Now asteroid mining catches my fancy and I believe it can only be the way to make us an interplanetary species.

After an educational tour, let’s go through the activities wherein I spend rest of my time. Coming to outdoor games, I like to play cricket, badminton, volleyball, and football. Cricket is my favorite. I have played for my schools and college. I have played some inter and intra-college tournaments. Apart from games, I love watching TV, documentaries, and cartoons. I watch documentaries related to astronomy, inventions, zoology, wild animals, wars, international-disputes and conspiracy theories. I watch about middle-east, sub-Saharan Africa, Russia, and Japan. I read a lot of articles, stories, news on that. But here I don’t have any political implications. You already know about my written works and I don’t think I need to explain it.

Where do I see myself after a period of time?

I would say probably on earth and better than what I am right now. I have deep research goals and have plans to continue my studies further. I would be happy if I get a chance to do some work related to asteroid mining and energy sector. I see my research works to be published in some of the best journals. Finally, I want to contribute something to humanity so that I may die with the feeling that I haven’t wasted the opportunity of being human, given by god.
